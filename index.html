What is a scam ?

A scam is when someone tries to trick you into giving them your money or personal information by pretending to be someone they're not. They may use phone calls, emails, or other methods to deceive you. For example, they might claim to be a government official, a bank representative, or a grandchild in need of money. They may use high-pressure tactics, create a sense of urgency, or offer false promises to gain your trust. Scammers target older adults because they may be more vulnerable or trusting. It's important to be cautious, verify information, and seek advice from a trusted family member, friend, or financial professional before taking any action or giving out personal information.

Types of scams

Scammers send fake emails, text messages, or create fake websites pretending to be a trustworthy entity, such as a bank, government agency, or a well-known company, to trick you into revealing personal information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or Social Security numbers.

Scammers create fake online profiles on dating websites or social media platforms and pretend to be interested in a romantic relationship to gain your trust and eventually ask for money or personal information.

Scammers promise high returns on investments or offer exclusive investment opportunities online, but in reality, they are trying to take your money without delivering the promised returns.

Scammers claim to be from the Social Security Administration and request personal information, such as Social Security numbers or bank account numbers, to gain unauthorized access or commit identity theft. create fake online profiles on dating websites or social media platforms and pretend to be interested in a romantic relationship to gain your trust and eventually ask for money or personal information.

Scammers pose as technical support representatives and contact you claiming that your computer has a problem or virus, and ask for remote access or payment to fix the issue, when in reality, they are trying to gain unauthorized access or steal your money.

Scammers send text messages pretending to be a trustworthy entity, such as a bank, and ask for personal information or prompt you to click on a link that leads to a fake website.

Scammers send text messages claiming that you have won a prize or gift card and ask for personal information or payment to claim the prize, which is usually nonexistent.

Scammers send text messages soliciting donations for fake charities or causes, and ask for payment or personal information to make the donation, but the donations end up in their pockets instead of going to a legitimate charitable organization.
